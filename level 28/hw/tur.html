The term "Tur" refers to two closely related species: the East Caucasian Tur (Capra cylindricornis) and the West Caucasian Tur (Capra caucasica). These animals are endemic to the Greater Caucasus mountain range, spanning Azerbaijan, Georgia, European Russia, Armenia, and parts of north-east Turkey. The East Caucasian Tur inhabits the eastern half of the range, while the West Caucasian Tur occupies the western regions. They are typically found at elevations between 1,000 and 4,000 meters, preferring steep, rocky slopes, cliffs, and alpine meadows.
