Blue whales have long, slender bodies with a blue-gray coloration, lighter underneath, sometimes appearing yellowish due to diatoms on their skin. They have a broad U-shaped head, small dorsal fin near the tail, and large flukes. Their mouths contain 70-800 baleen plates used to filter-feed on krill. The throat has 60-100 grooves allowing expansion during feeding. They have two blowholes capable of spouting water up to 12 meters high. Their hearts can weigh around 180 kg, comparable to a small car.
